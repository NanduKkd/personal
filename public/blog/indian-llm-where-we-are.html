Indian Foundation Models in 2026: A Data Snapshot

Scope: This is a benchmark-first snapshot focused on Sarvam-30B and Sarvam-105B using publicly reported numbers from model cards and public leaderboards.

1) Model release context

  • Sarvam-30B (MoE): released on Hugging Face, 2026-03-03.
  • Sarvam-105B (MoE): released on Hugging Face, 2026-03-03.
  • Both are positioned for multilingual Indian usage (22 Indian languages listed in model cards).

5) What the numbers say

  • Scale-up gain is clear: 105B improves strongly over 30B on agentic evaluations.
  • GLM-4.5-Air context: Sarvam-105B is ahead on LiveCodeBench and τ² Bench in the source table, while GLM-4.5-Air is ahead on SWE-bench Verified.
  • Open-model band is tight: GPT-OSS-120B, Qwen3-Next-80B, GLM-4.5-Air, and Sarvam-105B trade wins across benchmarks.

6) Positioning vs broader frontier landscape

On broad global aggregate leaderboards (e.g., Artificial Analysis), the current top cluster includes newer GPT-5.x, Gemini 3.1, and Claude 4.6-class systems. Sarvam’s strength is not “global #1 everywhere”; it is the combination of strong open-model capability plus India-focused multilingual alignment.
